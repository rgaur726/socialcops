Project 1 - What are the major health concerns in India?

Health

Number of people suffering from major diseases were used to find what are the most common forms of health issues our country is facing along. Diabetes, High BP and high blood glucose levels were the most common form of diseases for which people are admitted in hospitals. Interestingly, occupational risks was another major factor in the diseases of individuals.

Project 2 - Infant Health in India

New-borns and Children's Health in India

A comparasion study was done by me for the years 2013-14 and 2014-15 in the Health sector. Data regarding the total number of pregnant women in India for the tw years was taken, along with various other parameters related to delivery of the child. Then the data about the total number of children immunized before their first birthday was taken. Total number of infant deaths were also taken into account. The graph consisted of all these parameters for the two years.

Project 3 - Where are we spending our fuel?

India's energy consumption

Another comparasion study was done here for five years from 2008 to 2012. The top sources of energy present to us are Coal, natural gas, petroleum and renewable energy. How much of these are consumed per year is the major factor in deciding the economy of India since Fuel is a necessity. The results clearly proved that India is distinctily dependent on Coal for most of its energy needs. Maybe this is a sign that we really need to develop and find newer technologies for generation of power since we all know, Fuel is LIMITED.

Project 4 - Can India read and write?

Literacy rate of India

State-wise literacy rate of India was compared since Independence. Kerela as always emerged the most literate state of India. Mizoram and the National capital Delhi were among the top contenders too. J&k and Bihar lagged behind in the ability to read and write. A fascinating ovservation was that Union territories had a relative better literacy rate than the states, leading us with an interesting question: Can rule by a single person(The President) be better than a democracy?

Project 5 - Which language do Indians speak?

Languages spoken in India

A comparative study of the most common languages spoken by Indians. As expected, hindi was the most popular language. Bengali, Telugu, Marathi, Tamil were some distant seconds. India's wide variety of cultures and languages were cleary described from this particular study.

Project 6 - Is India getting warmer?

Temperature rise in India

Temperature data for the country for the past one century was taken to do an analysis of the average temperature rise in India and the results were quite daunting. Its not just that the temperature is rising, but the rise has been of almost 1 degree, showing how global warming has been detrimental to the climate.

Project 7 - When does India shop?

Timings of maximum orders by Indians

This was my personal favourite study in which I used data to check when does India shop the most? The number of completed orders and the number of returned orders were compared in the graph. Some very interesting insights were observed from the plot like the maximum of orders are placed between 6-7 in the evening. Another peak was observed near 9.30 to 10 A.M. in the morning showing that people do have a tendency to order stuff as soon as they reach their place of work.

Project 8 - Are Indians using wifi or cellular data?

Average data used on popular messenger apps

Another of my study which I really liked. Here, average wifi data was compared with cellular data used by Indians when using the most popular messaging apps like FB messenger, whatsapp, hike, BBM etc. Whatsapp was, not surprisingly, the most used messenger in India while all others were quite close together in the race.

Project 9 - Which state has the purest air?

State-wise forest cover of India

The geographical area of each state was compared with the forest cover of that state. The Seven sister states in north east India, along with sikkim, and some union territories turned out to be the most green state with the average forest cover almost close to the total geographical area of these states. Rajasthan, MP, and Maharastra were the worst in this regard.

Project 10 - FDI in India

Foreign Direct Investment in India

How much of investments are done by the FDI in Indian markets in the past four years was compared in this study. Interestingly, the FDI dropped to more than one-tenth of its level within the past 4 years which signals happy days for Indian businessmen.
